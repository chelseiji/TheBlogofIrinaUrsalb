How To Forget
01 - 10 - 10 P.F
93840238456
The Ministry of Data is always so damn cold.

The sterile environment I work in doesn't help. Blue lights from hundreds of screens project through the misted glass walls of the ministry. Data lights the endless corridors where I am meant to sort information and, more importantly, destroy it.

I'm currently cataloguing files "Pre-Freeze Folk Traditions of Harvest". So my entire day is looking at scans of people who are already long gone. However, my main task is deleting files about villages that have gotten iced over.

People at the Ministry keep disappearing. George and Anya from BioGenomics are gone, they've been assigned to "field work". This is usually code for they will never be seen again. They were alright coworkers, if you can even call them that, they were originally from mid-level blocks like mine. Probably got too close to something they shouldn't have, like those files on metabolic heating implants for infants (I may have read a couple of files in my spare time out of curiosity)

I quite liked them, I at least like them more than the others working here. The ones from families with connections and deeper pockets already, like Daria. If anxiety were a person, it would be Daria. Her parents had been initial supporters of the regime and had been compensated accordingly. So like all of the other rich kids in Zimnygrad, she was gifted a zapomnichip for her last birthday. Nothing says your family loves you more than a government-monitored implant that makes you a genius on test day but an emotional paranoid wreck every other day. She's been convinced that she is hearing voices talking about her through the chip, and to be fair, they probably are. I almost feel bad for her.

——

After months of working in this ice box, I finally found something I've been looking for, buried padlocked military logistics files disguised between various regular pre-freeze reports. A five-year plan from the year 05 P.F (prefreeze). The name, Project Eternal Winter.

Some secret team set jets and pumped the sky full of crystal-seeds to cause the Freeze to induce what they refer to as "a managed climate shift for centralized resource control and social unification."

At the bottom of the file: the official seal of the First Director. A beloved, frostbitten, genetically enhanced leader. The man who "saved" us from the very disaster he created.

I sat there, staring at the screen. I felt angry but wasn't allowed to show it. I felt the ghost of my dad near me with a profound told-you-so presence. I have memorized every code, every string, every digit of the file. I have built a perfect copy in my head, and I have sent the data to you, my sisters, in a variety of scrambled pieces, and then I deleted the original.

If they find out about this, I'm not getting reassigned. I'm becoming a very, very small pile of ashes.

I must be even more perfect now, more than I have had to be before. A diligent and uncurious archivist.
